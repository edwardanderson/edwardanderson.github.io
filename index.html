Edward Anderson

I'm a systems thinker who makes systems for thinking.

I work with cultural heritage people. Making tools, automating things.

Currently

Having enjoyed Visual History of European Alchemy at the Warburg Institute in London, I am now attending Seeing Green: Nature in Northern European Art, 1550–1680.

Previously

Between 2019 and 2025 I worked at the Rijksmuseum, where I built a semantic data layer to integrate two million heterogeneous records from its library, collection and archive into a knowledge graph. I worked with some great developers to build a Kubernetes-orchestrated event-driven microservices solution in the cloud. Added all-new APIs for search, harvest and discovery. All with lots of Linked Art, CIDOC-CRM, BIBFRAME, SKOS, PROV-O, Europeana Data Model, Records in Contexts and Dublin Core. Chris Dijkshoorn and I curated the CIDOC 2024 conference programme.

I taught Art on the Web: Creating Cultural Heritage Knowledge Networks at the 2024 edition of the Digital Art History Summer School at the University of Málaga.

I worked at the BFI National Archive for seven years, first as a non-fiction curator then as a software developer. I wrote and edited the feature-length documentary Around China with a Movie Camera in 2015, which screened at festivals around the world. Here are some notes about the archive featured in the travelogue, and an interview with me. I curated the Home Movies & Amateur Film (Pioneers of Personal Film, Ordinary and Extraordinary Lives, Amateur Auteurs) and China on Film (Beijing - Northern Capital, Shanghai - Pearl of the Orient, Hong Kong - Fragrant Harbour, Somewhere in China) collections for BFI Player. I interviewed Xie Fei for the the introduction.

Building data collection, enrichment and analysis tools for curators and researchers, I collaborated with Melanie Hoyes on The true picture for black actors in the UK film industry and with Dr Cath Sleeman on The gender imbalance in UK film casts and [...] crews. I made the first prototypes of the Britain on Film map in KML using NER and DBpedia.
